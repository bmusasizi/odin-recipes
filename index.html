Rolex Recipes

rolex

Ugandan Rolex, commonly referred to as Rolex, is a popular food item in Uganda, combining an egg omelette and veggies wrapped in a chapati. This single-portion dish is quick to prepare, and can be eaten at any time of the day, from breakfast to a lunch or supper meal or snack.

ingredients

  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
  • water (any temperature)
  • pinch of salt
  • rolling board and stick

    • rolex extras

  • 1-5 eggs (depending on your preference and love for cholesterol)
  • pinch of salt
  • small handful shredded cabbage
  • 3-4 thin slices of tomato
  • 3-5 thin rings of onion
  • avocado (as a bonus item)
  • cooking oil

steps

  1. Put the flour into a pot/pan, add a pinch of salt to the mixture.
  2. Slowly add water as you knead until the dough doesnâ€™t stick onto your fingers no more.
  3. Portion small balls of dough.
  4. Using a rolling stick, roll out the balls into a circular piece (large and thinly-rolled) of dough and roast it from both sides to your satisfaction

    5. extra steps

  5. Break egg(s) into a shallow cup, add pinch of salt and beat with a fork
  6. Start adding oil in intervals as you fry on the pan.
  7. Pour the eggs onto a large (preferably iron) saucepan into a thin circle
  8. Use a large spoon to rotate the eggs for even cooking. Flip once. Cook until the eggs are cooked through.
  9. Place the chapati on top and flip it all.
  10. With the chapati underneath the eggs warm together for 10-20 seconds.
  11. Remove egg-covered chapati from heat.
  12. Along the centre of the chapati (but to the right side) sprinkle a stripe of cabbage and slightly overlapped tomatoes and onion rings.
  13. Add sliced avocado for extra flavour.
  14. Sprinkle another pinch of salt over toppings.
  15. Fold the right side not covered in toppings to the other side along centre axis
  16. Enjooooooooy!
